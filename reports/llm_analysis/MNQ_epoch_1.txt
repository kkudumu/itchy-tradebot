**Ichimoku is the sole alpha generator â€” everything else is dead weight or a mixed bag on MNQ.**

**Key findings:**

1. **EMA Pullback is a net -231.8R drag.** Longs are the primary offender (-0.33R avg over 678 trades). Shorts are breakeven noise (-0.01R). The 43.4% WR on longs looks decent but the losses are outsized â€” stops are too tight relative to the trend continuation, causing frequent stop-outs followed by the trade moving in the intended direction. Disable entirely.

2. **FX at One Glance is directionally split.** Shorts are genuinely profitable (+0.58R, +262.7R total). Longs are destructive (-0.34R, -197.5R total). The strategy is a net +65R, but the long trades add ~-1.45R/trial of drag and inject drawdown variance that kills combine attempts. Solution: keep shorts, block longs.

3. **HTF Trend filter is completely inactive** â€” every trade shows `HTF Trend: ?`. MNQ lacks the 1H/4H indicator data that populates `htf_trend_1h`/`htf_trend_4h`. With `block_without_data=False` (default), the filter passes everything. This means counter-trend entries flow through unfiltered â€” explaining why EMA pullback longs and FXAOG longs are so destructive.

4. **Ichimoku is exceptionally strong.** Short signals at +2.40R with 48.7% WR across 197 trades is outstanding edge. Long signals at +0.64R with 86.5% WR are pristine but rare (37 trades = 0.27/trial). The ADX threshold at 28 is restrictive â€” lowering to 25 should generate ~15-20% more short signals while maintaining quality, since the R-multiple cushion is large.

5. **Projected impact of changes:** Removing ema_pullback (+1.71R/trial improvement) and blocking FXAOG longs (+1.45R/trial) brings per-trial expectancy from +2.43R to +5.58R â€” a **130% improvement**. Trade count drops from ~41 to ~25/trial (still adequate for a 22-day combine). Pass rate should jump from 38% to an estimated 55-65% as drawdown variance decreases.
