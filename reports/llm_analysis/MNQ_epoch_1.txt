### REASONING

**Ichimoku is carrying the entire system.** With +2.40 avg R on shorts (197 trades, 48.7% WR) and +0.64 avg R on longs (37 trades, 86.5% WR), it contributes ~+496R total. Everything else is a drag.

**ema_pullback is a net loser on MNQ.** Longs: 678 trades at -0.33R = ~-224R. Shorts: 803 trades at -0.01R = ~-8R. Combined: ~-232R across 1,481 trades. The 43.4% WR on longs with negative avg R means it's winning small and losing big â€” the R:R profile is inverted. With the default `rr_ratio: 2.0`, this means stops are being hit far more often than TPs, and the winning trades are closing early (likely via trailing or kijun exit) for less than 1R while the losers run the full stop. Disabling this strategy on MNQ is the highest-impact single change.

**fx_at_one_glance is marginally net positive** but only because shorts (+0.58R, 453 trades = +263R) outweigh the bad longs (-0.34R, 581 trades = -198R). Net ~+65R. The long side is the problem â€” same pattern as ema_pullback where longs bleed.

**HTF trend filtering is completely broken.** Every single row shows `HTF Trend: ?`, meaning `htf_trend_1h` and `htf_trend_4h` are both `None` in the edge context. The `TrendDirectionFilter.should_allow()` code returns `allowed=True` with "no HTF trend data â€” filter skipped" when both are missing. This is the root cause of the bad longs â€” without trend filtering, all three strategies take counter-trend longs freely. The recent commit mentions a fallback SMA trend was added but the indicator values aren't being populated into the edge context for MNQ.

**SSS strategy is absent** from the signal analysis â€” it's either disabled or not calibrated for MNQ (its defaults target XAUUSD with gold-specific pip values).

**The 38.2% pass rate is dragged down by ema_pullback's 1,481 losing trades.** Removing them and fixing trend filtering on the remaining strategies should materially increase pass rate.
