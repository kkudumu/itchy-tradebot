### REASONING

**The dominant signal: MCL has a massive long bias.** Every strategy's long side is profitable; every short side is a net loser (except marginally `asian_breakout` short at +0.32R).

**Longs â€” the edge:**
- `ema_pullback` long: 908 entries, 43.7% WR, **+1.86R** â€” workhorse; high volume, solid positive expectancy
- `ichimoku` long: 237 entries, 52.3% WR, **+2.44R** â€” best risk-adjusted edge on MCL
- `asian_breakout` long: 38 entries, 100% WR, **+7.88R** â€” extraordinary but tiny sample (38 trades across 166 trials = ~0.2/trial); treat as a bonus, not a pillar

**Shorts â€” the drag:**
- `ema_pullback` short: 786 entries, 26.6% WR, **-0.44R** â€” biggest P&L drain by volume. 786 losing trades at -0.44R â‰ˆ **-346R of cumulative damage** across all trials
- `ichimoku` short: 65 entries, 23.1% WR, **-0.61R** â€” small volume but worst per-trade expectancy
- `asian_breakout` short: 64 entries, 50.0% WR, +0.32R â€” marginally positive, net +20R; not worth the complexity

**Root cause: HTF Trend filter is non-functional for MCL.** Every row shows `HTF Trend: ?`, meaning no higher-timeframe trend data is being populated. The `trend_direction` edge filter has a `_block_without_data` attribute that was added (commit `f0beec9`) but **never wired into `should_allow()`** â€” it's a dead param. Result: all 851 short entries pass unfiltered.

**Estimated impact of blocking shorts:** Removing ~850 shorts at avg -0.40R saves ~340R of losses spread across 166 trials (~2R/trial improvement). With avg 36 trades/trial and the long-only pool being net positive, this should push the pass rate well above 50%.

**The 0.0% avg win rate in the trial summary** is a reporting artifact â€” individual strategy WRs are clearly non-zero (43.7%, 52.3%, etc.).
